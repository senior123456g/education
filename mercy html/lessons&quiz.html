Landforms Lessons and Questions

Landforms Lessons

Mountains

Mountains are large landforms that rise steeply above the surrounding land, typically having a peak or summit. They are formed by tectonic forces, volcanic activity, and erosion. Mountain ranges like the Himalayas and Andes are examples of mountain landforms.

Valleys

Valleys are low areas between hills or mountains, often formed by erosion from rivers and glaciers. The Grand Canyon and the Rhine Valley are examples of famous valleys.

Deserts

Deserts are barren, dry regions with little precipitation. They have unique ecosystems and features like sand dunes, oases, and rocky plateaus. The Sahara and Arabian Desert are two of the largest deserts in the world.

Plateaus

Plateaus are flat, elevated areas of land that have been uplifted due to tectonic movements or volcanic activity. The Colorado Plateau in the U.S. is a well-known plateau.
